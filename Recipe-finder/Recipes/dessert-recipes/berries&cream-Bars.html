Berries-and-Cream bars

Ingredients

servings 12

  1. 2 cups all-purpose flour
  2. 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  3. 3/4 teaspoon sea salt
  4. 10 tablespoons unsalted butter, at room temperature
  5. 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  6. 1 cup white sugar
  7. 3 large egg whites
  8. 2 teaspoons vanilla extract, divided
  9. 1/4 teaspoon almond extract
  10. 2/3 cup whole milk, at room temperature
  11. 1/2 cup plus 2/3 cup mixed berry jam or preserves, divided
  12. 1 cup whipping cream, chilled
  13. 1/4 cup marshmallow creme
  14. 1/2 cup freeze-dried strawberries or raspberries, for garnish

Directions

Step 1

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) and position rack in center. Lightly grease bottom of a 9-inch square cake pan. Line bottom with parchment paper. Grease and lightly flour parchment and sides of pan. Whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt in a medium bowl. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, mix butter and oil at medium speed until well combined, about 1 minute.

Step 2

Mix in sugar at medium-high speed until light and fluffy, 4 to 5 minutes. Mix in egg whites in 2 or 3 additions at medium speed until well combined, scraping bowl well after each addition. Mix in 1 teaspoon vanilla and the almond extract.

Step 3

Mix in about 1/3 of flour mixture at low speed. Mix in 1/3 cup milk; scrape bowl well. Repeat, alternating flour mixture and milk, until fully incorporated and batter is smooth.

Step 4

Spread batter in an even layer in prepared pan. Bake until a toothpick inserted into center comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs, about 30 minutes.

Step 5

Let cool in pan 10 minutes, then run an offset spatula or a knife around edge of cake and invert onto a wire rack. Remove parchment and let cool completely.

Step 6

Using a large serrated knife, halve cake horizontally to make two layers. Transfer one layer, cut side up, to a platter. Top with 1/2 cup jam, spreading evenly. Top with second cake layer, cut side down Meanwhile, for berry cream, purée remaining 2/3 cup jam in a blender or mini food processor until very smooth.

Step 7

In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a whisk attachment, beat cream at medium speed until it begins to thicken, about 1 minute. Mix in marshmallow creme at medium-high speed until soft peaks form. Mix in puréed jam and remaining 1 teaspoon vanilla at low speed until well combined. (If needed, fold whipped cream by hand to more evenly incorporate jam.) Frost top and sides of cake with berry cream.

Step 8

For garnish, crush freeze-dried berries with a mortar and pestle (or your hands) into a mixture of small pieces and powder; sprinkle over cake. Cut cake into 12 pieces. Chill in an airtight container until ready to serve, up to 2 days.
